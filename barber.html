Nissan combined its best technologies and produced “Skyline 2000GT-R” in February 1969 after merging with Prince Motors, Ltd. “GT-R” was a high performance car produced for touring car races. The know-how for prototype race car "R380" was filled in an ordinary looking sedan body*. Mounted was the 4-valve DOHC engine re-engineered from the one for R380 to suit production cars.

It made a debut and won at “JAF GP” in May 1969, 3 months after the rollout. Since then, it kept winning until it attained 52 victories before the Nissan works team withdrew in October 1972.

Racing History

The second-generation Skyline, the C110, was introduced at the 19th Tokyo Motor Show as the "Skyline 2000GT-R Racing Concept." The #73 was a reference to the fact that Nissan's works team planned to participate in 73 races in the 1973 season. But soon after, the company decided to pull out of motorsports to focus on supplying the booming demand for small cars following the first oil crisis. The show car was restored in 2007.
